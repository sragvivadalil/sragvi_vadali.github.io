Solar / Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicle

Objective: The objective was to design, create, and test a vehicular prototype that is fueled using a solar or hydrogen fuel cell as its power source while maximizing force and speed.

Course: Honors Principles of Engineering (POE)

Durantion: October 31, 2019 - November 13, 2019

Type: Circuits

In this project, my team and I needed to create a functioning vechile using a solar and hydrogen fuel cell. During this project, my main contribution was to help build the mechanism. Since there were many awards for the designs, one being a creative award, I explained that we should build a motorcycle shape. This is due to a motorcycle having a relatively light shape, making it easily move faster. In this project, I was the lead builder as for I explained to my teammates the shape and structure of the vehicle. Going through the design procress numerous times, we finally created a functioning vehicle. This project was difficult due to voltage and current outputted by the hydrogen cells. These cells give so little voltage, it is difficult for it to move a "heavy" vehicle. At the end, I also created a 2D simulation on the parts of the vehicle to test and present the values similar to our prototype. I did this by using a GUI software called Netlogo and I simulated the vehicle in its many configurations. I also worked to finalize the documentation.

Aerodynamic Design

Objective: The objective was to design, create, test, and conclude on a specific projectile using paper and tape to travel the longest distance.

Course: Honors Principles of Engineering (POE)

Durantion: January 9, 2020 - January 28, 2020

Type: Aerodynamics


In this project, my team and I needed to create a projectile that can travel a large distance. During this project, my main contribution was to create two designs of projectiles. For this project, we needed to create a projectile that can travel the farthest. This distance was tracked vertically. For my projectile, I decided to create a rocket. This rocket was created using printer paper and transparent packaging tape. When we were testing the rockets, I decided that 45 degrees was the most efficient angle to launch the rocket and it traveled 31 yards in 10 seconds at 60psi. This was due to the wind catching the rocket and caused it to slow down faster. Seeing my mistakes, I created another rocket similar to the previous rocket. I created the body using two papers, cardstock and printer paper and decided to decrease the fins size so that it doesn't catch onto the wind. I also wrapped the entire rocket with duct tape and increase the compression of the rocket. This rocket traveled 88 yards in approximately 3 seconds. Another aspect of this project is creating a 2d simulation using NetLogo. Using Aerodynamic equations, I was able to create a simulation for a rocket launch adding factors such as wind and initial velocity. This helped me find the best angle of launch, testing them in the virtual simulation. I also worked to finalize the documentation.
